§ 05

Control measures · Scope 1 & 2

Carbon reduction levers for MEP activities, organised by emission scope. Scope 1 addresses direct on-site combustion (generators, vehicles, welding gas); Scope 2 addresses purchased grid electricity used by tools, site offices, and installed systems.

Scope 1 · Direct

Direct emissions from MEP operations

Combustion at owned/operated sources — diesel generators, fuel pickup trucks, supplier transport, and on-site equipment.

i
Hybrid generators
Replace pure-diesel sets with hybrid diesel + battery systems; cuts idle-running fuel burn by 30–60%.
ii
Fuel-truck route & load optimisation
Consolidate pickups, schedule by site demand, eliminate empty runs.
iii
Supplier mobility shift
Replace single-use buses with carpooling, shared shuttles, or electric options where feasible.
iv
Low-carbon welding & cutting
Use power-efficient equipment for copper pipe cutting; reduce gas-fume emission & energy draw.
v
Equipment idling discipline
Mandatory shut-down protocol for compressors, generators & welders when not in active use.
vi
Preventive maintenance
Tuned engines burn less; quarterly emission checks on plant & pickup fleet.
Scope 2 · Indirect

Indirect emissions from purchased energy

Grid electricity consumed by MEP tools, site offices, lighting, HVAC during installation and ultimately by the installed systems themselves.

i
Energy-efficient MEP equipment
Specify high-rating HVAC, lighting and pumps with EPD/efficiency labels.
ii
Right-sized design
Second-pass load review prevents HVAC and electrical oversizing — the single biggest hidden-energy cost.
iii
Smart controls & BMS
Occupancy sensors, time-of-day scheduling, demand-controlled ventilation.
iv
Copper wiring & low-loss circuits
Higher conductivity → lower I²R losses → less grid draw over the asset's lifetime.
v
Site-power hygiene
LED site lighting, sub-metering, power-down policies for site offices & tool rooms.
vi
Renewables / green tariff
Procure green electricity contracts or pilot solar PV for site offices & storage yards.

§ 06

ESG systems & frameworks

A structured Environmental · Social · Governance architecture that anchors every MEP decision to measurable disclosure standards. The three pillars below convert the Sustainability Committee's commitments into auditable data, aligned with internationally recognised reporting frameworks.

Environmental

Environmental

Reducing operational & embodied carbon footprint of MEP activities.

  • Carbon emissions — Scope 1 & 2 measurement across diesel plant, fleet & purchased electricity.
  • Energy efficiency — HVAC right-sizing, BMS controls, LED & smart metering.
  • Material sustainability — Low-carbon ducts, plumbing, copper wiring with EPD verification.
  • Waste & circularity — Segregation, packaging reduction, offcut reuse register.
  • Water stewardship — Efficient plumbing systems & site water management.
  • Air quality — Power-efficient cutting/welding to reduce fume emissions.
Social

Social

People, workforce & community impact across MEP execution.

  • Health & safety — Reduced fume exposure from power-efficient welding/cutting equipment.
  • Workforce training — Sustainable installation toolbox talks for all MEP subcontractors.
  • Labour welfare — Energy-efficient transport replacing conventional buses for supplier mobility.
  • Subcontractor compliance — Environmental & safety standards enforced via onboarding.
  • Skills development — Upskilling teams on low-carbon materials and smart controls.
  • Community impact — Reduced site noise, emissions & traffic from optimised operations.
Governance

Governance

Oversight, accountability & ethical supply-chain management.

  • Sustainability Committee — Quarterly tracker with planned / ongoing / completed status.
  • Supplier pre-qualification — Weekly audits & inspections for compliance.
  • Procurement policy — Mandatory low-carbon material specification on all bids.
  • Transparency & reporting — Annual Scope 1 & 2 reduction report.
  • Risk management — Carbon, energy & supply-chain risk registers maintained.
  • Ethics & integrity — Documented MoMs, action ownership, audit trails.
